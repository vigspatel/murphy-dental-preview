## Preventing Complications from Third Molar Impaction

Wisdom teeth, or third molars, are the last to emerge and frequently lack the necessary space to erupt properly. This often leads to impaction, where the tooth remains trapped at an angle against the jawbone or neighboring roots. If left unaddressed, these teeth can trigger intense localized pain, develop cysts, or create hard-to-clean pockets that invite gum disease.

By choosing to remove these molars early, usually in the late teens or early twenties, you prevent the shifting of your existing dental alignment and avoid the systemic risks associated with chronic oral inflammation. Our focus is on identifying these potential issues through digital mapping before they become emergency situations.
