## Revitalizing the Foundation of Your Smile

When a tooth is lost or extracted, the surrounding bone in the jaw often begins to recede or "melt away" due to a lack of stimulation. This loss of height and width in the alveolar ridge can leave an unsightly indentation and, more importantly, may leave the jaw too thin to support a dental implant.

Ridge augmentation is a sophisticated regenerative procedure that reverses this atrophy. By carefully placing bone grafting material into the empty socket or along the recessed area, we stimulate the body’s natural ability to produce new bone. This process not only preserves your facial contours but also recreates the sturdy, healthy environment necessary for long-term restorative success.

## Precision Engineering for Your Jawbone

Building back lost bone density involves creating a biological scaffold where your body can regenerate its own tissue. During the procedure, Dr. Bulsara expertly introduces specialized mineralized material into the recessed areas of the jaw to serve as a blueprint for new growth. To give the bone-forming cells the quiet environment they need to thrive, we shield the site with ultra-thin, protective barriers that prevent faster-growing soft tissue from encroaching on the space.

This strategic intervention transforms a narrow, thinned ridge into a robust platform, providing the necessary depth and width to anchor a titanium implant securely. The result is a physically reconstructed foundation that allows your final replacement tooth to emerge from the gums with the stability and appearance of a natural root.
